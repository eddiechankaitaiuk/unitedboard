Staff Development
 
 

In July 2022, we conducted an Employee Engagement Survey to better understand the United Board’s staff climate and to strengthen our organizational culture. We wanted to identify our strengths, address our challenges, and identify some specific actions for improvement. The survey, considered together with employee exit interviews, gave us important feedback that we are using to improve our organizational culture.

We started conducting regular team meetings, retreats, and informal meals to build camaraderie and the spirit of unity. We have also revamped processes that assure all staff participate and have an opportunity to share their feedback with the senior team. Our goal is to build trust and transparency, to be even more collaborative in the service of our mission.

 
 

To help in these efforts, especially for our management team, we have hired Open Consulting, an HR development organization based out of Delhi. The coaches—Vivek Chopra, Sangeeta Chopra, and Ruby Thapar—have extensive experience working with NGOs and educational institutions. Each of them is skilled and perfectly suited to:

 
  • Help the team transition from being followers to leaders
  • Empower the team to use their strengths to inspire, influence, and energize others
  • Create psychological safety among team members and encourage open expression of views
  • Nurture new talent across the three locations
  • Infuse a sense of “one team” and foster collaboration by working on collaborative projects across the organization
 

This six-month-long program began in early January with strength assessments and self-reflection exercises for the entire staff, 360’s and ongoing one-on-one coaching for the nine senior leaders in order to enhance, sharpen, and further develop their skills. Staff participated in a two-day in-person workshop to enhance awareness of self and of others through creating connections, enhancing collaboration, and having meaningful conversations. They are currently working on cross-functional team projects to deepen collaboration with periodic check-ins with me and their coaches. We will report on the result of this work at the June Study Trip.

Staffing Updates
 
 

We are currently interviewing finalists for the Educational Technology Officer. This is an important position for us both internally, given our technology needs, as well as externally, as the impact of technology on higher education grows and our programming tries to keep pace.

The search for a Vice President for Programs (VPP) is on pause and I will continue to serve in the role as VPP and President. In the past months we have worked together well, collaborated across offices, and have functioned at a very high level. This arrangement will help maintain our current momentum, streamline our operations, and help flatten the organization’s structure. The request for me to continue in this capacity came from the program directors themselves after the staff development workshop. After consulting with the Board Chair, the Program Committee Chair, and the senior team, I have agreed. Going forward, I believe I can fulfil these responsibilities, provided I have the necessary support. After much deliberation, we have found this support in Ms. Rachel Zhang, who has been Program Officer for Leadership Development. She has a strong track record in international education development, cross-programming, and multi-location operations. She is a quick learner, has the necessary disposition, and she understands the United Board. Rachel will serve as Assistant Director for Strategy and Operations and will directly report to me. We will review this arrangement after two years and adjust as needed.

While I will continue to manage my other reporting lines, Finance and Administration will now report directly to Mr. Ricky Cheng. His strength in this area will add much and will help balance some of my workload.

Also, Ms. Jane Chan has successfully completed her probation period and has shown significant capacity and potential for growth. Since 1 March 2024, Jane has served as Program Manager.

Thank you for your continued support and guidance. Working together we are discerning how best to direct our energy and focus our resources in the service of Christian higher education in Asia.
